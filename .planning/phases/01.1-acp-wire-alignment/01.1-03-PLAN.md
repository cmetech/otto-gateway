<objective>
Bring the `session/new` and `session/prompt` wire shapes into spec compliance and surface the agent's stop reason as a typed canonical value (CONTEXT.md D-10..D-15, D-04, D-02, D-07, D-11..D-13). Vertical slice: a `NewSession()` call produces a spec-shaped request, surfaces the available models, and supports `result.id` fallback; a `Prompt()` call sends `prompt` (with defensive `content` duplicate), translates blocks into spec-compliant wire shape (text uses `text`, resource_link includes required `name`), and surfaces the agent's `stopReason` to the caller as a typed `canonical.StopReason` on `Stream.Result()`.

Purpose: Phase 1 sent `params.blocks` (kiro-cli ignored — empty prompts on every call), used `content` for text blocks (wrong field name), omitted required `name` on resource_link, and ignored `result.stopReason`. Phase 2 hard-depends on all four behaviors. The new `parseStopReason` helper, the `wireBlock` shape, and the `Stream.Result()` extension are introduced together so callers see one coherent fix.

Output: Replaced `sessionNewParams` / `sessionNewResult` / `promptParams` shapes in `client.go`. Replaced `wireBlock` shape and rewritten `translateBlock` switch in `translate.go`. New unexported helpers `firstNonEmpty` and `parseStopReason` in `translate.go` (per §Claude's Discretion — kept in `translate.go` rather than a new `parse.go` for file-scoped-layer consistency with Phase 1). New `FinalResult.StopReason` field in `stream.go` with merge-on-close semantics. Whitebox tests covering: NewSession surfaces models + id fallback; Prompt round-trips stopReason via Stream.Result(); translateBlock populates resource_link `name` (explicit + `path.Base` fallback); `parseStopReason` mapping table.
</objective>
